SPIRAL WOUND GASKET

Spiral Wound Gaskets (SWGs) are high-performance metallic sealing elements designed for critical flange applications involving high temperature, high pressure, thermal cycling, vibration, and chemically aggressive media. These gaskets are engineered for applications where conventional non-metallic sealing materials are unable to maintain long-term sealing integrity under demanding operating conditions.

The gasket is manufactured by precision winding a V-shaped metallic strip together with a soft filler material such as graphite, PTFE, or mica in a controlled spiral configuration. This alternating metal–filler structure provides an optimum combination of strength, flexibility, and recovery, ensuring reliable sealing performance under fluctuating pressure and temperature conditions.

The metallic winding provides structural reinforcement and blowout resistance, while the soft filler ensures effective sealing by conforming to flange surface irregularities. This results in consistent sealing stress retention throughout thermal expansion, pressure cycling, and mechanical vibration.

Spiral Wound Gaskets are widely used across oil & gas, petrochemical, power generation, chemical processing, marine, and heavy industrial applications where safety and sealing reliability are critical.
